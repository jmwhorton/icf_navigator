Learn more

Where can I find more information about this clinical trial?

A description of this clinical trial will be available on http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov, as required by U.S. law. This website will not include information that can identify you. At most, the website will include a summary of the results. You can search this website any time.

What if I have questions?

Please call the head researcher of the study ([PI Name]) at [PI phone] if you:
  • Have any questions about this study
  • Have questions about your rights
  • Feel you have been injured in any way by being in this study

You can also call the UAMS Institutional Review Board. This is the office at UAMS that supervises research. You may want to call them if you cannot reach the study team or want to speak to someone not directly involved with this study. To do so, call 501-686-5667 during normal work hours.

Your decision about joining the study

What are the alternatives to being in this study?

Alternatives are other treatments you can get or other ways you can help with the research besides being in this study.

You do not have to be in this study.

There are no alternatives to being in the study. This is because the study does not involve any treatment or other procedures that will help you. You will still be able to get medical care and treatment (as directed by your regular doctors) if you do not join this study.

What if I say no, I do not want to be in this study or do not qualify?

It is okay to say no to joining this study. You will not lose any services, benefits, or rights you would normally have at [insert study site] if you decide not to join. If you decide to join the study, it should be because you really want to volunteer.

If you are a student at [8.2, study site], nothing about your academic status will change, no matter what you decide.

If you are an employee at [8.2, study site], nothing about your employment status will change, no matter what you decide.

What happens if I say yes and join the study, but change my mind later?

You can stop being in the study at any time. You will still get regular medical care from your usual doctors if you stop being in the study.
